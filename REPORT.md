# CMAPSS FD001 — RUL prediction report

Generated by `run_project.py` on NASA CMAPSS subset FD001 (single operating condition, single fault).

## Setup

- **Piecewise RUL cap:** 125 cycles (training target and test labels for scoring).
- **Validation:** 20% of training engines held out (no leakage across cycles of the same engine).
- **Temporal model:** 1D CNN on sliding windows of length **30**; shorter test trajectories are front-padded by repeating the first observed row (standard pragmatic fix).
- **Tabular baselines:** settings + 21 sensors at each time step; `engine_id` and `cycle` excluded from features.

## Discussion

On validation, the 1D CNN sits between linear regression and the random forest, suggesting the temporal window does carry useful degradation structure. On the held-out **test** engines, the CNN underperforms the random forest (higher MAE/RMSE), which is plausible with a small architecture, fixed window length, front-padding for short tests, and full-data CNN training without engine-aware early stopping. Treat the CNN as a first temporal baseline; tuning windows, depth, regularization, and training protocol should be the next step.

## Methods (short)

Training RUL is `min(max_cycle − cycle, RUL_cap)`. Sklearn pipelines use median imputation and standard scaling. The CNN sees standardized windows per feature (scaler fit on training engines only).
